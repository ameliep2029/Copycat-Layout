How to Start Day Trading for Women: by Amelie Proietti

For women/girls who want to learn how to day trade, but don't know where to start! 18+ (PS. It's never to late or to early to start)


Before you start i want you to Understand the Risks and Potential for severe losses: Due to market volatility and the use of leverage (borrowed funds), you can lose more than your initial investment. The biggest risk of all is not taking one at all.

High Stress and Emotional Pressure: The fast pace requires quick decision-making under pressure, which can lead to impulsive and poor decisions driven by fear or greed.
Intense Competition: Individual traders compete against large financial institutions and high-frequency trading (HFT) algorithms, which have significant resource and technological advantages.
Pattern Day Trader (PDT) Rule: In the U.S., if you make four or more day trades within five business days in a margin account, you may be classified as a pattern day trader and required to maintain a minimum account balance of $25,000.
Tax Implications: Day trading profits are typically considered short-term capital gains and taxed at a higher ordinary income rate, rather than the lower long-term capital gains rate.

Foundational Pricipales for Beginners, Key Risk Management Techniques and Common Mistakes to Avoid.

Identify Risks: Proactively look for potential threats and opportunities that could impact your objectives. Analyze Risks: Assess the nature of the risk and who is involved. Control Risks: Create action plans to minimize the likelihood and impact of identified risks. Finance Risks: Determine how to pay for potential losses, often through insurance or setting aside reserves. Monitor and Review: Continuously track risks and update your strategy as they change over time.
